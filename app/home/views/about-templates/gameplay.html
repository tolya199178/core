Gameplay

Shadowcore is designed as a VR-only game, requiring an HMD and two handheld controllers. The main controller is used for aiming and shooting, while the off-hand controller is used for interacting with the environment and positioning your shield. Movement uses a teleport system to reduce motion sickness.

The teleport reticle can be positioned anywhere in the world you can see. It’s a green circle if the destination is a valid position and a red X if it’s not. Tapping a button on the main controller triggers the teleport. Teleporting is virtually instantaneous—a brief blur—but comes with a cooldown. It takes 1-2 seconds for your suit to recharge so you can teleport again.You aim your weapon with the main controller. Each character class has two weapon types to cycle through, giving you different options for different tactical situations. You have infinite ammo, but limited clip size, making timing reloading an important part of gameplay. Every time you shoot, your teleport timer resets so you can’t just pop out in the middle of an attack.Your off-hand controller is used for grabbing things in the world. At the most basic this means simple interactions like opening doors or pulling levers. However, you can also grab objects to carry them as improvised ballistic shields. Pick up a trash can or a manhole cover and use it to block or deflect incoming shots!

Scattered around each level are four types of power-up:

Armor

Regenerate your armor

Shield

An indestructible shield

Damage Boost

2x damage from all attacks

Speed Boost

Cuts your movement cooldown in half

These power-ups last for about 30 seconds after you pick them up. They respawn on a timer after they’ve been picked up, so you can grab them again if you return to their spawn point.
